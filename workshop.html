Short Event Description

This workshop explores the innovative use of haptic feedback and sensory exploration in educational robotics. Focusing on vibration modules integrated into circular robotic platforms, participants will delve into how tactile sensations can enhance learning experiences and engagement in STEM education. The workshop will provide hands-on opportunities to program and experiment with haptic technologies, fostering an understanding of how sensory feedback can be leveraged to improve comprehension of complex concepts, facilitate inclusive education, and stimulate interest in robotics among diverse student populations.

Detailed Event Description

The integration of haptic feedback in educational robotics offers a unique avenue for enhancing sensory engagement and making abstract concepts tangible for learners. This workshop aims to bring together educators, researchers, and practitioners to explore the potentials of using vibration modules in circular robots as tools for teaching and learning.

Participants will engage in interactive sessions covering:

  • Fundamentals of Haptic Technology: Understanding the principles behind haptic feedback and its applications in robotics.
  • Programming Vibration Modules: Hands-on activities to program and control vibration patterns in robots.
  • Designing Educational Activities: Developing lesson plans and activities that incorporate haptic feedback to enhance learning.
  • Inclusivity and Accessibility: Exploring how haptic technology can support learners with different needs, including those with visual impairments or learning difficulties.

Through collaborative projects and discussions, attendees will examine how sensory experiences can aid in teaching programming logic, computational thinking, and problem-solving skills. By the end of the workshop, participants will have practical knowledge and resources to incorporate haptic feedback into their educational practices, contributing to more engaging and effective STEM education.

Relevance and Impact

As robotics continues to play a pivotal role in education, incorporating sensory modalities such as haptic feedback can significantly enhance student engagement and understanding. Haptic technology bridges the gap between digital information and physical experience, making learning more interactive and accessible. This workshop addresses the underexplored area of sensory engagement in educational robotics, offering insights into how haptic feedback can cater to diverse learning styles and needs.

By focusing on practical applications and inclusive strategies, the workshop aims to impact the way educators approach teaching robotics and programming, ultimately contributing to the advancement of STEM education and fostering a more inclusive learning environment.

Workshop Format

The workshop will consist of:

  1. Presentations:
    • Invited talks by leading experts on haptic technology and educational robotics.
  2. Hands-on Programming Sessions:
    • Guided activities where participants program vibration modules in robots.
  3. Interactive Demonstrations:
    • Showcases of educational tools and applications utilizing haptic feedback.
  4. Panel Discussion:
    • A forum on the role of sensory feedback in inclusive education.
  5. Breakout Sessions:
    • Small group discussions on designing haptic-based educational activities.
  6. Poster Session:
    • Participants present research or projects related to haptic feedback in education.
  7. Remote Participation:
    • Live streaming of presentations and interactive Q&A sessions for virtual attendees.

Tentative Schedule

  1. Welcome and Introduction – 15 minutes
  2. Keynote Presentation: The Role of Haptics in Education – 30 minutes
  3. Expert Talk: Programming Vibration Modules – 30 minutes
  4. Hands-on Session I: Basics of Haptic Programming – 45 minutes
  5. Break – 15 minutes
  6. Panel Discussion: Inclusivity through Sensory Feedback – 45 minutes
  7. Hands-on Session II: Designing Educational Activities – 45 minutes
  8. Poster Session and Networking – 45 minutes
  9. Closing Remarks and Future Directions – 15 minutes
